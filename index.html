Summary

I am a third-year Electrical and Computer Engineering student at the University of Washington focused on embedded systems, digital hardware design, and hardware–software co-design. My work spans FPGA development in SystemVerilog, microcontroller programming with Arduino, PCB design and fabrication, sensor integration, motor control, and system-level debugging of real-time systems.

My interests center on building reliable, experimentally validated systems that bridge theory and physical implementation. I enjoy prototyping, iterative testing, and debugging across electrical, firmware, and timing constraints, and I value clean system architecture, reproducibility, and clear documentation in collaborative environments. I am particularly interested in research and project work related to embedded intelligence, robotics, digital systems, and scalable hardware architectures, with the goal of developing practical technologies that perform robustly in real-world conditions.
